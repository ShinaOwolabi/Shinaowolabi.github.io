The Scientist
Behind the Work

A career built at the intersection of rigorous research and practical animal science.

I am Owolabi Shina James, RAS, a Registered Animal Scientist, educator, researcher, and community development advocate based in Ibadan, Nigeria. My work sits at the meeting point of rigorous science, practical field experience, and a genuine commitment to using knowledge to improve lives.

Academically, I hold a First Class Honours B.Tech in Animal Production from the Federal University of Technology Minna, a professional diploma in Science Education, and an MSc in Animal Science and Agricultural Biochemistry from the University of Ibadan, completed in April 2025. Across every level of study, I graduated top of my class, earning multiple national scholarships and commendation awards along the way.

"Science without purpose is incomplete. I research, write, and teach because I believe knowledge applied well changes communities."

My research career spans over 12 peer-reviewed publications in animal nutrition, poultry science, and agricultural biochemistry. I currently serve as an Analytical Representative at Bluespectrum Chemical Lab Ltd, where I lead laboratory research, design data validation protocols, and organise scientific training workshops. I also serve as a Research Coordinator at RAISSAT and as an Associate Research Fellow at The Sanguine Academy, contributing to high-impact manuscripts and collaborative agricultural science projects across Africa.

In education, I spent two years as a Teach For Nigeria Fellow, teaching Agricultural Science in underserved secondary schools in Ogun State and mentoring students toward careers in agriculture. That experience deepened my understanding that education is the most sustainable investment any society makes.

My voluntary work reflects the same conviction. Through the Dreams to Legacy Initiative, I serve as Volunteer Coordinator and Facilitator, mentoring over 200 Nigerian youths on accessing quality education and career opportunities. I also served as Project Manager for a community development service that engaged two secondary schools on the future of agriculture. I am an active member of the Baptist Students Fellowship alumni network, where I organised academic mentorship sessions for over 500 students during my undergraduate years.

Outside the lab and the classroom, I follow the Sustainable Development Goals, particularly Zero Hunger, with genuine personal investment. I believe precision nutrition, sustainable feed formulation, and evidence-based agricultural education are among the most direct paths toward food security in Africa.
